3D Printer

Rotation 5 is based on 3D printing and scanning. That's why I'm going to give a quick introduction to what are your possibilities with 3D printers.

3D printers have the awesome capacity of being able to create (almost) anything you can come to mind with and makeing tests. The only limitations to 3D printing are the size and the precission, obviously the file you are trieing to print can't be bigger than the 3D printer itself and it can't be too small either because every 3D printer has a level of precission, wich is the size height that each printed layer has. There are different types of 3D printers, we have the classic FDM wich stands for Fusion Deposition Modeling, and the more advanced SLA with stands for Stereolithography. FDM works by melting material, while SLA works by hardening resine with lasers. Our school has 5 MakerBot's 5th Gen, one FormLabs (SLA) 3D printer and one RepRap X400 3D Printer.
